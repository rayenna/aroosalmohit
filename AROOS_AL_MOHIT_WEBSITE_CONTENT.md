### SECTION: What is Ship Chartering?

**Section label:** THE SERVICE

**Section title:** What We Do

**Body:**
Ship chartering is the process by which a cargo owner or trader secures the exclusive use of a vessel — or part of a vessel — for the transportation of goods. Aroos al Mohit acts as your professional intermediary throughout this process: we source suitable tonnage, negotiate the commercial terms on your behalf, and manage the fixture through to completion.

Our value lies not just in finding a ship, but in finding the *right* ship, at the *right* price, on the *right* terms — and then ensuring every word of the charter party protects your interests.

---

### SECTION: Charter Types

**Section label:** CHARTER TYPES

**Section title:** Tailored to Your Requirement

**Sub-text:**
Every cargo is different. Every trade has its nuances. We advise on the most appropriate charter structure for your specific situation.

---

**Charter Type 1: Voyage Charter**
A vessel is engaged for a single voyage from a loading port to a discharging port. The shipowner bears the operating costs; the charterer pays a freight rate per tonne or a lump sum. Ideal for one-off or opportunistic cargo movements.
*Best for: Spot cargoes, trial shipments, irregular trade flows*

**Charter Type 2: Time Charter**
The charterer hires the vessel for a defined period — weeks, months or years — and pays a daily hire rate. The charterer controls the commercial deployment of the vessel during that period.
*Best for: Regular cargo programmes, seasonal requirements, trading flexibility*

**Charter Type 3: Bareboat Charter**
The charterer takes full operational control of the vessel, including crewing and maintenance. The owner provides only the hull. Long-term arrangements only.
*Best for: Operators seeking effective ownership without capital outlay*

**Charter Type 4: Contract of Affreightment (COA)**
A long-term agreement to carry a series of cargoes between agreed ports over an agreed period, using vessels nominated as required. Provides volume commitment with operational flexibility.
*Best for: Regular bulk traders, fertilizer importers, grain traders*

### SECTION: Our Chartering Process

**Section label:** HOW IT WORKS

**Section title:** A Disciplined Process from Brief to Completion

**Step 1 — Cargo Brief**
You provide us with your cargo details: commodity, quantity, loading and discharging ports, laycan window, and any special requirements. The more detail, the faster we move.

**Step 2 — Market Search & Vessel Screening**
We simultaneously canvass our private owner network and the open market, screening vessels for suitability — age, flag, class, trading history, cargo capability and owner reputation.

**Step 3 — Shortlisting & Presentation**
We present you with a shortlist of suitable vessels with indicative freight rates and vessel particulars, along with our market commentary and recommendation.

**Step 4 — Negotiation**
We negotiate directly with owners or their brokers — freight rate, laycan, loading/discharging terms, demurrage rate, cargo exclusions and all other commercial points — until we have the best achievable terms.

**Step 5 — Charter Party Review & Fixture**
We review the full charter party or fixture recap, clause by clause, to ensure your interests are protected. Once agreed, we confirm the fixture formally.

**Step 6 — Post-Fixture Support**
We remain your point of contact through the voyage — available for NOR tendering queries, laytime calculations, demurrage disputes and any operational issues that arise.

**Body:**
Beyond individual fixtures, Aroos al Mohit offers advisory services to companies that need deeper, more sustained maritime expertise. Whether you're evaluating a freight hedging strategy, reviewing a charter party dispute, or building a shipping procurement programme from scratch, our team brings the market depth and commercial acumen to guide you.

---

**Advisory Service 1: Freight Market Analysis**
Regular written market commentary on dry bulk, tanker and general cargo freight markets — Baltic index movements, regional supply-demand dynamics, seasonal trends and forward outlook. Tailored to your trading desk's specific commodity and route exposures.

**Advisory Service 2: Voyage Estimation & TCE Analysis**

We assist cargo owners in accurately calculating laytime, monitoring statement of facts, preparing or defending demurrage claims, and negotiating demurrage settlements with shipowners — reducing leakage on every voyage.

**Advisory Service 5: Shipping Procurement Strategy**
For companies with regular cargo programmes, we advise on optimal procurement strategy: spot vs. COA, voyage vs. time charter, risk allocation, and freight budgeting — building a shipping programme that delivers consistency and cost control.
